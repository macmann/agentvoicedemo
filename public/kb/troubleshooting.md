# Home Internet Troubleshooting KB

## Router has no lights
- Check that the router power cable is firmly connected to both the router and wall outlet.
- Try a different wall outlet and confirm the outlet has power.
- Press the power button on the router if available.
- Wait 60 seconds to see if indicator lights return.

### Escalation criteria
- No lights after power and outlet checks.
- Router emits unusual sounds or smell.

## Red or blinking internet light
- Confirm all WAN/internet cables are firmly seated on both ends.
- Restart the router: unplug power for 30 seconds, then reconnect.
- Wait up to 3 minutes for the internet light to stabilize.
- If available, check ONT/modem status lights and ensure they are normal.

### Escalation criteria
- Internet light remains red after restart and cable check.
- ONT/modem shows alarm or loss-of-signal.

## Wi-Fi connected but no internet
- Forget and reconnect to the Wi-Fi network on one device.
- Test a second device to confirm whether all devices are affected.
- Restart the router and wait for lights to stabilize.
- If one device only is affected, reboot that device and disable VPN temporarily.

### Escalation criteria
- Multiple devices have no internet after reboot and reconnect.
- Captive portal/authentication repeatedly fails.

## Slow or unstable connection
- Move closer to the router and test connection speed again.
- Pause high-bandwidth downloads/streaming on other devices.
- Restart the router and retest after 3 minutes.
- If possible, test via ethernet to isolate Wi-Fi interference.

### Escalation criteria
- Connection remains unstable after restart and proximity test.
- Frequent drops continue across wired and wireless tests.

## Escalation criteria
- Issue persists after 4 troubleshooting steps.
- User requests agent escalation at any time.
